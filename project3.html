The Challenge

Traditional hand-carved mahjong, once a cherished craft, faces a rapid decline in Hong Kong due to the rise of machine-made mahjong and cheaper mainland imports. With fewer artisans and diminishing demand, preserving this intangible cultural heritage has become a significant challenge.

The Solution

Mr. Ko Yun-kan, one of Hong Kong's last hand-carved mahjong masters, has adapted his business to modern demands. By incorporating machine-made mahjong into his offerings and exporting to diverse markets, he has sustained his store's legacy. Simultaneously, he innovates by turning handmade mahjong into customizable souvenirs, appealing to younger generations and ensuring this timeless craft remains relevant.
